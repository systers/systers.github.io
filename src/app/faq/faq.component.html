Frequently Asked Questions

Q1) I am new to open source. Where do I start?
A good start would be to look at all the projects on the Systers GitHub page and find one that fits your skillset best. If you still can't find a match, just mention @maintainers on Slack and someone will help you.
Q2) I see a lot of projects have been archived. Which projects are active right now?
We have several active projects going on: VMS, Portal, PLT, PC-Prep Kit, Powerup (Android & iOS) are all active and need work. If you want to work on an archived project, you can fork it and work on it independently. Your contributions will still be recognised.
Q3) I picked out a project. What do I do next?
Each project comes with a README which guides you on how to setup the project. If you face any problems you need to head over to the project’s slack channel and ask your question there. Someone from the community will help you out. If you need help right away, please mention @maintainers and someone will help you out. Once the project is setup, read the contributing guidelines to make your first contribution.
Q4) I want to participate with Systers in GSoC/RGSoC. How do I start?
We have open sessions related to different FOSS programs and also about specific projects. Attending atleast open session is mandatory for your participation and is also recommended as it gives you a chance to learn about us and also get any questions answered.
Q5) Which PRs/Issues do I review as a Mentor/Maintainer?
All of them.
Q6) Which PRs/Issues do I review as a Newcomer?
All of them.
Q7) Where do we discuss ideas related to the project development?
In the project channel (i.e. #gh-pages, #vms, etc.)
Q8) What is the minimal technical knowledge needed to contribute to Systers projects?
Zero technical knowledge needed! Because you can always help with documentation, testing the app, and creating wireframes to update it to the new branding guidelines. i.e. Reaching out to therapist for PowerUp scenario content, reviewed UI designs for Wheelchairs from the Cerebral Palsy communities on Facebook.
Q9) Can I contribute to GSoC projects even if I’m not a GSoC student?
Yes, GSoC projects are discussed in public channels because we want community involvement. You can even attend weekly meetings of each project if you want to! These projects are just as open source as they were before the GSoC selections. You can contribute with ideas, and even issues if they're not labeled as GSoC issues.
